Gender Bias and Workforce

How Does Gender Affect Workplace? How Can It Affect Careers and the Pay Gap?

Gender Pay Gap

Did you know that women are paid only 87 percent of what men are paid?

That means that for every dollar a man is paid, a woman with the same job and position is paid only 87 cents.

The "Broken Rung" Penalty

The "Broken Rung" Penalty refers to a recurring trend. For every 100 men promoted to manager, only 93 women are. Only 48% of women in male-dominated workplaces feel women are treated fairly in promotions

Hiring Bias

Due to stereotypes and biases, women are less likely to be hired. In fact, women are four times more likely than men to report being treated as if they were not competent because of their gender (23% of women vs. 6% of men).

Leadership Underrepresentation

While they are significantly less hired, they are not given many leadership positions either. Women hold only 29% of C-suite positions, and this drops to 16-24% in industries like engineering, manufacturing, and energy.

Harassment and Bias

In some cases, women might be harassed or bullied in the workplaces due to their gender. Shockingly, 41% of women have experienced sexual harassment in the workplace in the past 5 years eve though the efforts to stop it are persistent.
