Linked SDG Goals with Gender Equality

This page connects the concept of Gender Equality to other SDGs,
to expand the understanding of this concept.

What effect does linking Gender Equality to other SDGs have?

Nowadays, the global issue of Gender Equality is crucial for the daily lives of all human beings. Linking UN SDG number 5 to other SDGs creates a powerful ripple effect, because improving gender equality helps accelerate progress in areas like education, health, economic growth, and poverty reduction. When women and girls have equal access to opportunities and resources, communities become healthier, more productive, and more inclusive — making overall development faster and more sustainable.

Explore the Connections

SDG #1 — No Poverty

Promoting gender equality allows women equal access to jobs, income, and resources. When women can participate fully in the economy, they contribute directly to household earnings, reducing financial vulnerability. This uplift in income and resource access helps lift entire families out of poverty and raises overall living standards across communities.

SDG #4 — Quality Education

Ensuring equal rights for girls leads to higher school enrollment and completion rates. When social and legal barriers to girls' education are removed, more young women gain the skills and credentials needed to pursue meaningful opportunities. This educational foundation not only benefits the individual but also generates long-term returns for families and societies that depend on an informed and capable workforce.

SDG #8 — Decent Work and Economic Growth

When women have equal access to employment and fair wages, they contribute more substantially to the broader economy. Closing gender gaps in labor markets increases overall workforce participation and productivity. Countries and communities that harness the full economic potential of women consistently demonstrate stronger and more sustainable patterns of economic growth.

SDG #10 — Reduced Inequality

Gender equality directly addresses one of the most pervasive forms of structural discrimination. By dismantling barriers that prevent women from accessing the same rights, opportunities, and protections as men, societies move toward greater fairness. This reduction in gender-based disparity contributes to a broader culture of inclusion, making it easier to address other forms of inequality rooted in race, class, or geography.

SDG #11 — Sustainable Cities and Communities

Empowering women to participate in civic life and urban planning leads to more inclusive decision-making processes. When women have a voice in how cities and communities are designed and governed, the resulting policies tend to prioritize safety, accessibility, and shared public spaces. This broader representation produces environments that are more functional and equitable for all residents, not just those who traditionally held planning authority.
